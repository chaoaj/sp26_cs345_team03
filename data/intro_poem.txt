Your nightmares have been terrible tonight
Dreams of a life in a world warped by metal forged by false gods
Who put you to work in the heavens
Inside a tower of Babel in which you don't realize the words written mean nothing
You lay in bed beneath a night sky who's stars are obscured by the light pollution of their substitutions
Who's flames shine with no warmth
Who's constellations are woven by your demiurges, across 65 million miles
So that none might stray so far that Plato, weeping, can lead them out of the cave
Far beneath your spot in the stack of little boxes that houses all your nameless neighbors
Constructed beasts roar, their forms shaped from the bones of stars, fueled by the harvested souls of ancient ancestors
Their endless march drowns down into the sound of ocean waves you've never seen 
They lull you deeper into your cold cushions as you drift awake
